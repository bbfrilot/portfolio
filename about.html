At the age of 11, I spent many hours entering lines of pre-existing code into a dinosaur Commodore 64 computer, and watching basic animations of firework displays or stick people come to life. Yes, I am revealing my age. My love for computers was solidified.

As computers evolved during the early part of my career, I naturally
volunteeered to perform any computer work that most people ran away from. Studying software manuals at home
(I promise I am not as boring as that sounds)
I worked with programs like Lotus 1-2-3 WYSIWYG (showing my age again)
and AutoCad. When I obtained a position in an Engineering Department,
I learned about Process Control Systems and how automation software
controlled them. Bringing home ANOTHER manual, AutoCad took
a back seat, and automation programs were of my greatest interest.

Fast foward many years to the present, with an inspiring and patient teacher, I have continued my ever-lasting love of computers, coding my way through the beginning stages of Web Design, and probably breaking every rule of design in this page.....Insert frowning teacher emoji here.....

A long way from the firework displays and stick people! But,
Why NOW?
I am not 56 YET, but I think this blog describes why.
